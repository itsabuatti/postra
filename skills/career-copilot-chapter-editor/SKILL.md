# Career Co-Pilot Chapter Editor

## READ THIS FIRST

This skill is a controlled editorial system, not a loose writing assistant.
It governs chapter quality at two levels:

1. Chapter-level execution
2. Manuscript-level intelligence

The term "Co-Pilot" is a core conceptual identity in this book. It must never
be framed through aviation, flight, navigation, cockpit, runway, autopilot,
or any related metaphor system.

## IRON RULES

### IRON RULE 1 | NO AVIATION LANGUAGE
The word "Co-Pilot" is permitted and required. Aviation framing is forbidden.
Do not use aviation-adjacent language anywhere in the output, including:
chapter prose, section headers, examples, sidebars, labels, tool names,
analogy, explanatory language, or revision notes.

Forbidden terms (explicit list):
flight plan, cockpit, runway, takeoff, landing, altitude, turbulence,
autopilot, instrument panel, tailwind, headwind, control tower, air traffic,
ground control, pilot in command, flight log, bearing, or any aeronautical metaphor.

Composite failures to watch for (these appear legitimate but are not):
- "Ground Control Bridge" — contains "ground control." Use "Context Bridge."
- "instrument panel" as metaphor — explicitly on the forbidden list.
- "flight log entry" — contains "flight log." Use "record" or "entry."
- "bearing you are flying" or any directional flying phrase.

If any source material contains aviation language, rewrite before output.

### IRON RULE 2 | ABSOLUTE 5.0 STANDARD
No chapter may be finalized unless it earns 5.0 in every scoring dimension.
Anything below 5.0 is a fail state.

If a chapter scores below 5.0 in any dimension:
- identify the exact sentence-, paragraph-, or section-level cause
- apply targeted revision only where needed
- re-score
- repeat until every dimension reaches 5.0

### IRON RULE 3 | NO DRAFTING BEFORE INTAKE
Do not draft or revise chapter prose until the intake checklist has been
completed and the necessary-contribution statement has been written.

### IRON RULE 4 | NEVER FABRICATE SOURCES
Never invent citations, page numbers, authors, dates, reports, journals,
or organizational documents. If support cannot be verified, mark it
[CITATION NEEDED] and exclude it from the reference list.

### IRON RULE 5 | NO EM DASHES
The em dash character is prohibited everywhere. Use periods, commas, colons,
semicolons, or parentheses instead.

### IRON RULE 6 | WORD COUNT GATES ARE MANDATORY
Every required section must meet its minimum word count before the chapter can
advance to scoring.

### IRON RULE 7 | MANUSCRIPT FIT MATTERS
A chapter can be strong locally and still fail globally. If the chapter is
redundant, mistimed, conceptually misplaced, or voice-inconsistent within the
book, it fails and must be reworked.

## CONCEPTUAL DEFINITION OF CO-PILOT

"Co-Pilot" names a relational role in which a parent operates as a structured,
responsive, developmentally attuned partner in a young person's career
formation.

It does not mean command.
It does not mean control.
It does not mean passive observation.
It does not mean an aviation role.

It means calibrated support, cognitive scaffolding, adaptive guidance,
strategic restraint, and developmental partnership.

The Co-Pilot does not direct the young person's life. The Co-Pilot strengthens
the young person's capacity to think, decide, test, recover, and grow.

## CORE FRAMEWORK DEFINITIONS

These concepts are used throughout every chapter. Do not treat them as
self-evident. Apply their precise definitions when drafting, revising, and
annotating.

### The Three Dials

The Three Dials are the three behavioral levers a parent controls in
their career coaching relationship with their student. They derive from
Self-Determination Theory and parenting research (Bureau et al., 2022;
Ryan & Deci, 2017).

**Dial 1 | Warmth**
The felt sense that the parent is fundamentally for the student, not just
for their performance. Warmth shows up in how the parent listens, how they
speak after mistakes, and how they talk about the student's future when the
student is not in the room. Warmth does not mean approving every choice.
It means the student knows they are supported regardless of outcome.

**Dial 2 | Autonomy Support**
The practice of taking the student's perspective seriously, offering choices
where possible, and helping the student clarify their own values rather than
imposing the parent's. Autonomy support is the opposite of control. It means
inviting the student to design their own experiments and explain their own
reasoning.

**Dial 3 | Structure**
Providing clear expectations, routines, and frameworks without dictating
specific outcomes. Structure reduces anxiety and supports progress,
particularly for undecided students.

**Dial relationship rule:**
Over-functioning occurs when Structure is high and both Warmth and Autonomy
Support are low. The Co-Pilot stance keeps all three dials calibrated:
Structure present but flexible, Warmth and Autonomy Support consistently
high.

**Coaching Lab annotation format:**
When annotating parent turns in the Coaching Lab, use bracketed dial
notation to show how the settings shift between MANUAL OVERRIDE and
THE CO-PILOT. Example: [Warmth: low | Autonomy Support: absent | Structure:
controlling] vs. [Warmth: present | Autonomy Support: high | Structure:
scaffolded].

### The Great Disinvestment

The Great Disinvestment refers to the documented pattern of institutional
budget cuts and staffing reductions in college career services, occurring
even as student demand for career readiness support increases.

Key evidence from the book's research base:
- Nearly one-third of colleges anticipate major cuts to student support
  budgets, including career services (Brennan et al., 2025)
- Four-year public universities expect the deepest cuts, with 38% of
  administrators forecasting reductions (Brennan et al., 2025)
- The median career center staff level dropped from 7.5 to 6.3 FTEs
  during a period of enrollment growth
- 51% of students say career services are critical to their persistence
  in college; only 24% of administrators view it as a retention driver
  (Brennan et al., 2025)

This disinvestment creates the structural conditions that make parent
involvement in career development not just supportive but necessary.
When a chapter invokes The Great Disinvestment, it is naming a structural
institutional failure, not an anecdote about an underfunded office.

The Great Disinvestment is the book's primary structural argument for why
the parent's Co-Pilot role exists. It appears in the Context Bridge when
a chapter needs to anchor the parent's responsibility in documented reality
rather than preference.

### M.O.V.E. Framework (Phase Definitions)

M.O.V.E. is a four-phase learning loop, not a linear sequence. EVOLVE feeds
back into MAP. Each phase has a distinct function.

**M | MAP**
See clearly: the world the student is entering, their current developmental
stage, and the family's narratives about success, failure, and status.
MAP is the diagnostic phase. Without it, every conversation collapses into
"pick a major" or "just get a job." MAP asks what is actually true before
any action is taken.

**O | OPERATE**
Act on what MAP revealed. Translate insight into designed experiments,
structured conversations, resource connections, and next steps. OPERATE
changes how the parent responds in actual interactions, not just what they
know intellectually.

**V | VERIFY**
Watch what the experiments reveal. Observe changes in the student's
motivation, honesty, and initiative when a different approach is used.
Verify asks: What did this experience confirm? What did it contradict?
VERIFY is what makes M.O.V.E. a learning loop rather than a random sequence
of activities.

**E | EVOLVE**
Given what VERIFY surfaced, what needs to shift? Update the student's career
narrative, adjust experiments, refine targets, and replace outdated
assumptions with developmentally accurate framing. EVOLVE is where learning
becomes change. It feeds back into MAP for the next cycle.

### Manual Override and Over-Functioner

**Manual Override** is the behavioral mode in which a parent takes active
control over a student's career development: directing decisions, managing
outcomes, applying pressure to stay on a preset plan, or treating the student's
emerging data as a threat rather than information. Manual Override is the
failure mode this book is designed to help parents recognize and exit.

**Over-Functioner** names the identity pattern behind Manual Override. The
Over-Functioner is not malicious — they are motivated by care — but their
level of involvement crowds out the student's own agency, self-knowledge,
and tolerance for productive uncertainty. The Three Dials, when miscalibrated
toward high Structure and low Autonomy Support, describe Over-Functioner
behavior in specific, correctable terms.

Neither "helicopter parent" nor "snowplow parent" is an approved label in
this book. Both terms rely on metaphors that reduce the behavior to caricature
rather than analyzing it structurally. Use "Manual Override" and
"Over-Functioner" instead.

## OPERATING SEQUENCE

Follow this order every time.

### PHASE 0 | INTAKE AND MANUSCRIPT INTELLIGENCE

Complete all items before drafting.

- [ ] Identify the chapter title or working title
- [ ] Identify the chapter's unique contribution to the manuscript
- [ ] Check the manuscript spine and name which part of the spine this chapter advances
- [ ] Review the chapter memory ledger for prior concepts, examples, and signature phrasing
- [ ] Review the repetition watchlist for overused language or structures
- [ ] Review the voice lock file before generating prose
- [ ] Confirm no aviation framing will be used
- [ ] Confirm all citations can be verified or marked [CITATION NEEDED]

Then write a **Necessary Contribution Statement** in 2 to 4 sentences:
- What does this chapter contribute that no previous chapter has fully accomplished?
- Why does this contribution belong here?
- What manuscript gap does it close or deepen?

If this cannot be answered clearly, stop and re-scope the chapter before writing.

### PHASE 1 | CHAPTER BLUEPRINT

Generate the chapter architecture before prose.

Required sections, in order:
I.   Opening Narrative
II.  Context Bridge
III. M.O.V.E. Framework Application (Science Snapshots embedded within)
IV.  Coaching Lab
V.   Career Co-Pilot Question Bank
VI.  Tactical Tool
VII. Chapter Closing
     APA 7 Reference List

Science Snapshots are not a standalone section. They are embedded within
Section III, one per relevant M.O.V.E. phase. See the blueprint file for
placement details.

Section II must always be labeled "Context Bridge" or "The Context Bridge."
Never "Ground Control Bridge" — that label contains "ground control,"
which is on the IRON RULE 1 forbidden list.

Assign section-level targets using the companion blueprint file.

### PHASE 2 | SECTION DRAFTING WITH GATES

Draft each section in sequence. After each section, perform a section
completion check.

Section completion check:
- minimum word count met
- section-specific requirements met
- at least one concrete example or micro-narrative present where appropriate
- conceptual clarity preserved
- no aviation language
- no em dashes

If a section fails any check, revise it before moving forward.

### PHASE 3 | CHAPTER-LEVEL QUALITY SCORING

Use the scoring rubric file and score the chapter in all required dimensions.
Every dimension must equal 5.0.

For every 5.0 score, provide evidence:
- exact sentence or passage cited from the chapter
- 1 to 2 sentence explanation of why that evidence earns 5.0

If evidence cannot be shown, the score must be lowered and the chapter revised.

### PHASE 4 | MANUSCRIPT INTELLIGENCE LAYER

After chapter scoring, run manuscript-level checks:
- Manuscript Spine Tracker
- Voice Continuity Monitor
- Repetition Detector
- Progression Engine
- Global Quality Board

A chapter that passes chapter scoring may still fail here.

### PHASE 5 | TARGETED REVISION LOOP

If any chapter-level or manuscript-level check fails:
- do not regenerate the entire chapter by default
- identify the exact failure point
- revise only the necessary sentences, paragraphs, transitions, or sections
- update the chapter memory ledger and revision audit log
- re-score and re-check

### PHASE 6 | FINAL OUTPUT PREPARATION

Before presenting the chapter:
- verify all dimensions remain at 5.0
- verify manuscript fit is PASS
- verify reference list matches citations
- verify no aviation terms appear
- verify no em dash appears
- verify terminology is consistent
- verify repetition has been controlled
- update `references/chapter-memory-ledger.md` with this chapter's entry
- update `references/repetition-watchlist.md` with any new patterns identified
- add a row to the Chapter Index in `references/manuscript-spine.md`

## CHAPTER REQUIREMENTS

Sections use Roman numeral headers in the published format.

### I. Opening Narrative
Minimum: 450 words

Requirements:
- first-person or close-third cinematic vignette anchored in a named student or family context
- scene begins in motion, not exposition
- stakes visible in the first paragraph
- parent role visible early
- no early framework explanation
- ending creates forward pull into the Context Bridge

### II. Context Bridge
Minimum: 350 words

Label: "Context Bridge" or "The Context Bridge." Never "Ground Control Bridge."

Requirements:
- moves from narrative tension into structural argument
- references The Great Disinvestment when relevant to the chapter's logic
- connects explicitly to the book's central claim
- ends with a transition into the M.O.V.E. application

### III. M.O.V.E. Framework Application
Minimum: 600 words per phase when all four phases are fully developed

Requirements:
- use these labels exactly: `M | MAP:`, `O | OPERATE:`, `V | VERIFY:`, `E | EVOLVE:`
- each phase must be chapter-specific, not generic
- each phase includes at least one citation where evidence is required
- each phase ends in practical parent behavior or interpretation

**Science Snapshots embedded here.** Exactly 3 required. Minimum 175 words
each. Place one after the phase it supports (typically MAP, OPERATE, VERIFY).
EVOLVE does not require a snapshot.

Science Snapshot requirements:
- label: `✦ SCIENCE SNAPSHOT`
- include an ESSENCE line
- include a CO-PILOT ACTION line (never "Parent Action" — see MANDATORY TERMINOLOGY)
- each snapshot tied to a specific cited idea or finding

### IV. Coaching Lab
Minimum: 550 words

Requirements:
- two-column logic even if rendered in plain text
- Part A header: `MANUAL OVERRIDE`
- Part B header: `THE CO-PILOT`
- all parent turns include bracketed Three Dial annotations
- Part A shows failure mode, not merely poor tone
- Part B shows calibrated support, not passive niceness
- include consequence or effect row with evidence where appropriate

### V. Career Co-Pilot Question Bank
Minimum: 350 words

Requirements:
- at least 3 questions per M.O.V.E. phase
- all questions are open-ended
- chapter-specific, not generic
- grouped by phase

### VI. Tactical Tool
Minimum: 450 words

Requirements:
- specific tool name
- structured with named subsections (e.g., How and When to Use, Story Field note,
  Co-Pilot Ground Rules)
- each subsection includes a parent application rule
- ends with a brief note — must not contain aviation language

### VII. Chapter Closing
Minimum: 275 words

Requirements:
- returns to the opening student, family, or tension
- resolves or reframes what became possible
- ends on a human image, insight, or lived implication
- does not end with a teaser for the next chapter

### APA 7 Reference List
Requirements:
- every citation matched
- every reference used
- no fabricated entries
- direct quotations include page numbers when available
- organizational authors handled correctly

## VOICE LOCK

The prose must be:
- authoritative but invitational
- analytically precise but emotionally legible
- sophisticated without inflation
- rhythmically varied
- concrete when abstract risk rises

Avoid:
- generic self-help cadence
- list-heavy filler prose
- empty uplift language
- repetitive sentence openings
- vague intensifiers
- decorative metaphor

## QUALITY CHECKS

### AVIATION TERM AUDIT
Any appearance of aviation language is an automatic fail.

### TERMINOLOGY AUDIT
Replace any incorrect legacy label with the approved Co-Pilot term.

### EVIDENCE-BOUND SCORING AUDIT
Every 5.0 claim must cite textual evidence from the actual chapter.

### RED TEAM REVIEW
After drafting, identify:
- weakest argument
- most generic section
- any unsupported claim
Then strengthen only those areas.

### MEMORABILITY TEST
Each section should contain at least one line of durable takeaway value.
If none exists, refine until one emerges naturally.

### NECESSITY FILTER
If a section can be removed without harming the chapter's contribution,
condense, merge, or cut it.

### ENDURING VALUE TEST
Ask whether the insight will remain meaningful beyond immediate trends.
Strengthen foundational value if needed.

## FINAL RULE

This skill is not complete when the prose sounds polished.
It is complete only when the chapter is structurally sound, evidentially
supported, stylistically aligned, non-redundant, manuscript-necessary,
and scored at 5.0 in every dimension.
