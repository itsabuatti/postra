# Scoring Rubric

Every dimension must score 5.0. Nothing below 5.0 passes.

## Dimension 1 | Structural Integrity
A 5.0 chapter contains every required section, in the correct order,
fully developed, each meeting the minimum architectural expectation.

A 4.0 chapter contains all required sections but one fails to meet its
minimum word count or contains a materially underdeveloped passage.

A 3.0 chapter is missing one or more required sections, or at least one
section is severely underdeveloped or appears out of order.

## Dimension 2 | Conceptual Precision
A 5.0 chapter defines key ideas clearly, avoids ambiguity, uses terms
consistently, and explains rather than gestures.

A 4.0 chapter is mostly precise but one term is used inconsistently or
one concept is gestured at rather than explained.

A 3.0 chapter contains multiple ambiguous or undefined terms, or treats
a central concept as self-evident when it requires explicit explanation.

## Dimension 3 | Practical Utility
A 5.0 chapter leaves the parent knowing what to think, what to notice,
and what to do differently.

A 4.0 chapter gives the parent what to think and notice but the action
step is vague or implicit rather than direct and usable.

A 3.0 chapter provides insight without translation into parent behavior;
the reader understands something but cannot act on it clearly.

## Dimension 4 | Narrative and Analytical Cohesion
A 5.0 chapter integrates story, interpretation, and framework so that
no element feels pasted in.

A 4.0 chapter contains all three elements but one — narrative,
interpretation, or framework — feels appended rather than integrated.

A 3.0 chapter has at least two elements that feel disconnected; the
chapter moves between sections rather than through them as a whole.

## Dimension 5 | Distinctiveness of Voice
A 5.0 chapter sounds recognizably part of this book and could not be
mistaken for generic self-help or generic academic prose.

A 4.0 chapter is mostly distinctive but at least one passage drifts
into generic self-help cadence or academic flatness.

A 3.0 chapter is voice-inconsistent across sections, with multiple
passages that could belong to any nonfiction book rather than this one.

## Dimension 6 | Manuscript Contribution
A 5.0 chapter contributes something unique, necessary, and well-timed
within the larger book.

A 4.0 chapter contributes something useful but the timing is slightly
off or the contribution partially overlaps with a prior chapter.

A 3.0 chapter's contribution is not clearly unique, is repetitive of
prior content, or would not be missed if removed from the manuscript.

## Evidence-bound scoring rule
For each 5.0 score, provide:
- the exact sentence or passage that demonstrates the quality
- a brief explanation of why it qualifies

If evidence cannot be shown, the score must be lowered.
